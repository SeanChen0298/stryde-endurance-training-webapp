Plan overview v3 · 6 Mar 2025

This 24-week plan follows a polarised training model with a 80/20 intensity split: 80% of weekly volume at easy aerobic effort (Zone 1–2) and 20% at threshold or above. Given your current aerobic base of ~55 km/week and an HRV baseline of 55ms, the plan prioritises building your long-run capacity through weeks 14–18 before transitioning into race-specific sharpening.

Your lactate threshold pace is estimated at 5:28–5:38/km based on recent tempo efforts. The goal pace of 6:03/km represents ~90% of threshold — achievable if your long-run endurance (aerobic ceiling) continues its current trajectory. Two quality sessions per week (one tempo, one interval) are scheduled in weeks 14–19, reducing to one in the taper.

Malaysia heat is factored throughout: all long runs and quality sessions are scheduled for 5:30–7:00am. Pace targets include a +5–8s/km heat allowance for weekend runs in Penang. Body battery >70 is required for quality sessions — if it dips below this, the session downgrades to easy automatically.
